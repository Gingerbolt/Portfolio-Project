About Me

Just the same old story of the kid who wanted to do some good and studied an area that would not further that goal.

Following acquiring my degree in criminology and my subsequent distaste for retributive justice I discovered that I could communicate well. So I became a salesman for a spell.

I sold plenty of insurance until the time came for my clients to use the policies I had sold them and the insurance company found ways to keep from paying out. I'd found another way to hurt people through my work.

I concluded that I would need to do the work myself. So I began to work for a landscaping company, taught myself and got full pesticide and herbicide certifications within a month. The work was satisfying. Eventually I had to move on because I felt the effects of pesticide over-exposure.

I knew that I needed to do my own work but I wanted to work in conjunction with a team. I knew I needed to do good. So another three months on and I had a certification as a residential energy analyst. Within six months I had a new job with a small company.

That job didn't last long because the company was unable to get enough sales to pay me a living wage. I got a new job, this time for the money.

I became a debt collector, working with the worst cases. I saved hundreds of people's homes and improved their lives all while making a tidy sum from the company. The work was temporary however, it eventually came to an end.

On the upside my newfound freedom allowed me to dive into working in politics. It was there that I really learned that my creativity needs to get out. I was given voter turnout goals and told to achieve them. Utilizing resources from across the state we worked to make calls into Nevada at a rate that has never been seen in any prior elections. Training and guiding the phone volunteers was a particular strength, they would always come back when I trained them say it was because I explained so well. The election ended and it's been like a treadmill I was on suddenly stopped.

That leaves me throwing my momentum here. Knowing I need to do my own work, and knowing that I thrive with a team, programming seems to be the perfect fit.
